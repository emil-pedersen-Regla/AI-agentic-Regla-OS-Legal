### ðŸ”´ RISK #3: Bad Leaver Provision - Total Loss at Par Value

**SA Clause:** 14.4 - "In the event of a Bad Leaver situation, the Company... is entitled... to purchase up to all shares of the relevant Party at par value."

**FA Terms:** No bad leaver provision mentioned

**Bad Leaver Definition (SA Clause 1.1):**
- Company terminates for material breach (including rightful dismissal/"bortvisning")
- Key Person terminates without Company's material breach
- **[OPTION highlighted]:** Termination within Cliff Period for ANY reason

**Risk Analysis:**

**Scenario 1: Termination for "Material Breach"**
- Term "material breach" is vague and could be interpreted broadly
- Could include: missing deadlines, disagreements on strategy, performance issues
- Even if you've worked 3.5 years of 4-year vesting (87.5% vested), you lose EVERYTHING

**Scenario 2: You Quit Without Company Breach**
- If burned out, want to pursue other opportunities, or have personal reasons to leave
- Even with shares 100% vested economically, you lose them at par value
- Company worth â‚¬10M, your 33.33% = â‚¬3.33M, you receive DKK 6,668 (â‚¬895)

**Scenario 3: "Bortvisning" (Rightful Dismissal)**
- Danish employment law allows summary dismissal for serious misconduct
- Standard bar: Theft, violence, serious insubordination, criminal acts
- BUT "material breach" could be interpreted more broadly in shareholder context
- Risk of dispute over whether conduct qualifies

**Market Comparison:**
- **Standard Market (Investors):** Bad leaver = unvested shares buyback at par OR nominal value
- **Standard Market (Investors):** Vested shares retained OR bought at fair market value
- **This SA:** ALL shares (vested + unvested) at par value
- **Assessment:** This provision is **extremely investor-favorable** and **extremely founder-unfavorable**

**Financial Impact Examples:**

| â‚¬3M | â‚¬1M | â‚¬895 | â‚¬999,105 (99.9%) |

**Why This Matters:**
- Creates massive downside risk even after years of successful work
- "Material breach" is subjective and could lead to disputes
- Disproportionate penalty - lose millions for relatively minor issues
- Eliminates economic value of vested shares earned through years of work

**Recommendation:** **REJECT bad leaver applies to vested shares.**

**Counter-Proposal:**
- **Bad Leaver = unvested shares only** at par value
