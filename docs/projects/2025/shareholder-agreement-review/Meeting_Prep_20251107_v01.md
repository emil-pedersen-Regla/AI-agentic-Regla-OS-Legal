### Issue #6: CEO Tiebreaker Removed

**Problem:** SA requires unanimity for material decisions; no tiebreaker mechanism
**FA Terms:** Section 5.1 - CEO breaks deadlock unless 2/3 override

**Your Position:**
"The FA gives me tiebreaker authority as CEO, overridable by two-thirds. The SA eliminates that and requires unanimity, which creates deadlock risk. For a startup to operate effectively, there needs to be a decision mechanism when we disagree."

**Martin May Say:** "Unanimity ensures all founders are aligned on important decisions."

**Your Response:**
"Alignment is good, but deadlock is fatal. What happens if we genuinely disagree on an important decision and can't reach unanimity? The company is paralyzed. The FA had a balanced solution - CEO breaks ties, but a supermajority can override the CEO. That protects against both deadlock and CEO unilateralism. We need that mechanism in the SA."

**Counter-Proposal:**
Restore FA provision:
- Qualified majority (2 of 3) for material decisions
- If deadlock (1-1-1), CEO decides
- BUT 2 of 3 can override CEO if needed

**Alternative:**
- Reduce unanimity list to truly extraordinary matters (sale of company, IPO, liquidation)
- All other material decisions: Qualified majority

**Red Line:** Will not accept pure unanimity with no tiebreaker mechanism
