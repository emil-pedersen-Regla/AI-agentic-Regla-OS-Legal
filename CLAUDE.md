#### Anti-Dilution Protection

**Full Ratchet (Investor-Favorable, Aggressive)**:
- **Mechanism**: If down round occurs, investor's conversion price adjusts to new lower price
- **Impact**: Investor receives additional shares to maintain investment value
- **Example**:
  * Invested â‚¬2M at â‚¬10/share = 200,000 shares
  * Down round at â‚¬5/share
  * Full ratchet adjustment: Investor now has 400,000 shares
  * **Founders massively diluted**
- **Negotiation Position**: Strongly resist; extremely punitive to founders

**Weighted Average (Balanced, Market Standard)**:
- **Mechanism**: Adjustment proportional to down round severity and amount raised
- **Types**:
  * **Broad-Based**: Includes all shares (more founder-friendly)
  * **Narrow-Based**: Includes only certain share classes
- **Nordic Standard**: Broad-based weighted average
- **Example**:
  * Down round impact much less severe than full ratchet
  * Shares added proportionally based on formula
- **Negotiation Position**: Broad-based weighted average is acceptable market standard

**No Anti-Dilution**:
- **Impact**: Investors share dilution equally with founders
- **Rare**: Only in founder-very-strong bargaining positions

#### Drag-Along Rights - Control Analysis

**Definition**: Majority shareholders can force minority shareholders to sell in acquisition

**Key Strategic Terms**:

**Threshold Percentage**:
- **60-70%**: Investor-favorable (allows investor control with smaller stake)
- **75-80%**: Nordic standard (requires supermajority)
- **85-90%**: Founder-favorable (difficult threshold to reach)

**Strategic Impact Analysis**:
- **60% threshold + Investor has 60%**:
  * Investors can force founders to sell against their will
  * Founders lose exit timing control
  * Acquisition price might not meet founder expectations

- **75% threshold + Investor has 60%**:
  * Requires founder cooperation for exit
  * Founders retain meaningful veto on bad deals
  * Balance of power more equitable

**Additional Protections**:
- **Minimum price threshold**: Sale must exceed certain valuation
- **Same terms**: Founders get same per-share price as investors
- **Reasonable advance notice**: 30-60 days to review and decide

**Nordic Market Standard**: 75-80% threshold is prevailing practice

**Negotiation Position**: Push for 75% minimum; 80% is founder-protective

#### Founder Vesting - Protection Analysis

**Standard Nordic Structure**:
- **Duration**: 4 years total
- **Cliff**: 1 year (no vesting until 12 months)
- **Schedule**: Monthly vesting after cliff
- **Example**: 1,000,000 shares

  * Month 13: 250,000 shares vest (cliff release + Month 13)
  * Months 14-48: 20,833 shares/month

**Acceleration Clauses - Critical Strategic Term**:

**Single-Trigger Acceleration**:
- **Trigger**: Acquisition alone causes vesting acceleration
- **Impact**: Shares vest immediately upon acquisition
- **Acquirer Risk**: Acquirer inherits fully-vested founders
- **Rare**: Most acquirers reject single-trigger
- **Negotiation**: Difficult to achieve unless strong leverage

**Double-Trigger Acceleration** (Nordic Standard):
- **Triggers**: Acquisition AND termination without cause
- **Impact**: Protects founders from post-acquisition termination without shares
- **Percentage**:
  * 25-50%: Conservative
  * 50-75%: Common
  * 75-100%: Founder-favorable
- **Nordic Standard**: 50% double-trigger is common
- **Negotiation Position**: Push for 75-100% double-trigger

**Strategic Analysis**:
- **No Acceleration**: Founder risks losing unvested shares in acquisition + termination
- **Double-Trigger 100%**: Founder fully protected if terminated post-acquisition
- **Example**:
  * 1M shares, 50% vested at acquisition
  * Post-acquisition termination:
    - No acceleration: Walk away with 500K shares (lose 500K)
    - 100% double-trigger: Walk away with 1M shares (full protection)

### Analysis Framework for Shareholder Agreements

#### Investment Terms
- Investment amount and pre/post-money valuation
- Share class (ordinary vs preference shares)
- Liquidation preference (1x, participating/non-participating)
- Anti-dilution provisions (full ratchet vs weighted average)
- Vesting schedules (4-year with 1-year cliff is standard)

#### Governance
- Board composition (founder seats, investor seats, independent)
- Voting rights (per-share vs class-based)
- Reserved matters (requiring supermajority or specific consent)
- Information rights (financial statements, board observer)
- Protective provisions (investor veto rights)

#### Exit Provisions
- Drag-along rights (can investors force sale?)
- Tag-along rights (can founders join investor sale?)
- Right of First Refusal (ROFR) on share transfers
- Co-sale rights (investor can sell alongside founders)
- Lock-up periods (restrictions on selling after exit)

#### Protective Provisions for Founders
- Founder vesting (are founders subject to vesting?)
- Non-compete and non-solicitation (how restrictive?)
- IP assignment (confirm Regla owns all IP)
- Confidentiality obligations
- Founder termination scenarios (what happens to shares?)

#### Economic Terms
- Dividend rights (preference vs ordinary)
- Distribution priorities (who gets paid first in exit)
- Conversion rights (can preference convert to ordinary?)
- Redemption rights (can investor demand buyback?)
- Participating preference (gets preference + pro-rata share)

#### Operational Clauses
- Representations and warranties (what is Regla representing?)
- Conditions precedent (what must happen before closing?)
- Covenants (ongoing obligations - e.g., provide financial statements)
- Indemnification (who indemnifies whom for what?)
- Dispute resolution (Danish courts? Arbitration?)
